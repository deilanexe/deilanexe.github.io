Research

My PhD dissertation thesis at RMIT University is:

Domain-Sensitive Topic Management in a Modular Conversational Agent Framework

My thesis involved the automatic construction of modular ontologies representing the vocabulary of a topic (e.g. zoos, museums, food). These ontologies were incorporated into a conversational system capable of engaging in chat-like dialogue with users. I also investigated the effect of conversational domains in the human perception and automatic measures of semantic relatedness.
My supervisors were Lawrence Cavedon, John Thangarajah and Wilson Wong.


My previous research topics were related with the Trading Agent Competition (TAC) in the Supply Chain Management (SCM) Game. The theses were:

For Masters (BUAP, Puebla, Mexico):

Desarrollo de Agentes Inteligentes para la Competencia TAC SCM (Development of Intelligent Agents for the TAC SCM Game)


And for Bachelor (UPAEP, Puebla, Mexico):

Diseño e Implementación de Agentes para el TAC SCM (Design and Implementation of Agents for the TAC SCM)


Both projects were under the supervision of Darnes Vilariño and Fabiola Lopez y Lopez.
