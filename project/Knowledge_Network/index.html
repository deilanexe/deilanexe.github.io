Knowledge Network (Ontology 2.0; Mar-Sep 2016)

 

One of the major challenges with the Sensis Ontology program was the scale of the proof-of-concept system against the final production version, which was smaller in comparison. Consequently, some of the tasks of the construction process that were originally implemented sequentially could not be completed even in finite time.

The second version of the Sensis Ontology, which was also rebranded as the “Knowledge Network”, consisted of re-thinking the original implementation that produced the database contents as tasks that could be executed in parallel. In addition to this, we reviewed and simplified some of these tasks to reduce dependencies and speed up the overall process to produce the network.

In the end, the process to construct the Knowledge Network passed from infinite time to less than six months (as with the original PoC), and a ten-fold increase in performance.
